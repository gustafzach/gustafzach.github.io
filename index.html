Abstract

The presented work examines the feasibility of coordinating a large-scale multi-agent system according to a time-varying probability density function using nature-inspired control techniques. The proposed method works in a decentralized manner where only information about neighboring agents' positions is required. The information is then used to estimate the local density, which is further used to generate a velocity command. Building upon previous work, a feedback law based on the heat equation is extended by a feed-forward term based on the continuity equation. This accommodates a velocity field driving the agents towards the desired time-varying spatial density. We demonstrate how the proposed technique can be used to track both simple and complex time-varying densities. The proposed control law is tested in a simulation to verify its performance.
